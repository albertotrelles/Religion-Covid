\section{DiD Design} 

\textbf{README}: The following document presents results for two DiD models (1) TWFE as in Brodeur et al. (2021)\footnote{Abel Brodeur, Andrew E. Clark, Sarah Fleche, Nattavudh Powdthavee, COVID-19, lockdowns and well-being: Evidence from Google Trends, Jornal of Public Economics, Volume 193,2021} and (2) Borusyak, Jaravel, and Spiess (2023)\footnote{Kirill Borusyak, Xavier Jaravel, and Jann Spiess. Revisiting Event Study Designs. Working Paper, 2023.}. It also reports pre-trends tests as suggested in Borusyak, Jaravel, and Spiess (2023). 
\vspace{1em}

3 different treatment definitions are used:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Lockdown Announcement}: Day in which a statewide lockdown is announced. (1 in tables, Blue in graphs)
    \item \textbf{Lockdown Start}: Day in which a city lockdown starts within a state. (2 in tables, Red in graphs)
    \item \textbf{Lockdown End}: 7 seven prior to the lockdown announcement. (3 in tables, Green in graphs)
\end{itemize}
\vspace{1em}

\textbf{TWFE}: The empirical strategy leverages the panel structure of the Google Trends data and the staggered implementation of COVID-19 lockdowns within a difference-in-differences framework. The daily frequency of the series enables us to incorporate 2019 observations to construct the counterfactual trajectory, in addition to never-treated and not-yet-treated units, as is standard DiD designs. As in \textcolor{red}{(well-being paper)}, we estimate the following. 

$$y_{it} = \gamma \mathcal{D}_{it} \times \mathds{1}_{2020} + \delta \mathcal{D}_{it} + \rho L(x_{it}) + \mu_i + \lambda_t + \varepsilon_{it}$$

where $y_{it}$ are Google searches for a specific topic of interest in state (country) $i$ at time $t$, $\mathds{1}_{2020}$ an indicator variable for year $2020$, $\mathcal{D}$ is a dummy equal to one since the day lockdown is announced in 2020 and its paired day from 2019. $L(x_{it})$ controls for one day lagged Covid-19 average deaths and average cases, $\mu_i$ are state (country) fixed effects, while $\lambda_{t}$ is composed of fixed effects for year, week (of year) and day of week (dow). The parameter of interest is $\gamma$, standard errors are clustered at the day of year level, and regressions are weighed by state's (country's) population.  
\vspace{1em} 


\textbf{Borusyak, Jaravel, and Spiess (2023)}: Within the set of observations $it \in \Omega$, we define the set of treated observations as $\Omega_1 = \{ it \in \Omega: D_{it}=1 \}$ and $\Omega_0 = \{ it \in \Omega: D_{it}=0 \}$ as the set of untreated observations (which include never-treated units).  We estimate the effect of Covid-19 lockdowns in three steps\footnote{Covid controls are not included in step one due to lack of variation and, most importantly, absence of high values. The latter makes the `out-of-sample' predictions unreliable when extrapolating for treated observations.}.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item For untreated observations $it \in \Omega_0$ and weighting by population, regress $y_{it} = \delta \mathcal{D}_{it} + \mu_i + \lambda_t + \varepsilon_{it}$  to estimate fixed effects
    \item For treated observations $it \in \Omega_1$, compute $\hat{\tau}_{it} = y_{it}-\hat{y}_{it}(0)$, where the last term comes from extrapolating the previous model to the treated subsample. 
    \item Aggregate for treated observations averaging $\hat{\tau}_w = \sum_{it \in \Omega_1} w_{i}\hat{\tau}_{it}$, where $w_i$ weights by population. 
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Pre-trends test}: As suggested in Borusyak, Jaravel, and Spiess (2023), for $it \in \Omega_0$ estimate $y_{it} = \sum_{k<0}\tau_k \mathcal{E}_{it}^k \times \mathds{1}_{2020} + \sum_{k<0}\delta_k \mathcal{E}_{it}^k + \delta \mathcal{D}_{it} + \mu_i + \lambda_t + \varepsilon_{it}$. We should expect $\tau_k$ to be non-significant. 

\vspace{2em} 
The following three sections present results for three different specifications of time fixed effects.

\begin{itemize}
    \item FE1: year, week-of-year, dow, state FEs 
    \item FE2: year, day of year (1-100), dow, state FEs
    \item FE3: year, state interacted with day of year (1-100), dow FEs  
\end{itemize}
